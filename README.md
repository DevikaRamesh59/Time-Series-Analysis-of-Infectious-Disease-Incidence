# Time-Series-Analysis-of-Infectious-Disease-Incidence (1980-2024)


##  Project Overview
This project is an **End-to-End Data Science Case Study** focused on analyzing the global trajectory of communicable diseases over four decades. Using a hybrid approach of **Python** (for data engineering) and **Power BI** (for business intelligence), I transformed raw, fragmented healthcare data into a strategic dashboard that highlights historical peaks, disease stabilization, and the impact of global health interventions.



---

## Technical Stack & Tools
* **Language:** Python 3.x
* **Libraries:** Pandas, NumPy, Seaborn, Matplotlib (for Advanced EDA)
* **BI Tool:** Power BI Desktop (Python Scripting Integration)
* **Dataset:** Global Communicable Disease Records (1980-2024)

---

## Data Science Workflow

### 1. Data Engineering & Pre-processing (Python)
* **Data Integrity:** Handled missing values and standardized column names for global compatibility.
* **Outlier Management:** Identified extreme spikes (e.g., the 1993 AIDS anomaly) and applied **Winsorization (Capping)** to maintain visual accuracy.
* **Statistical Scaling:** Applied **Log Transformation** to handle highly skewed incidence data, ensuring small-scale trends remained visible alongside large-scale outliers.

### 2. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
* **Univariate Analysis:** Evaluated individual disease frequencies and statistical distributions.
* **Multivariate Analysis:** Developed 3D and 4D visualizations to understand the complex correlation between **Time, Geography, and Disease Severity**.

### 3. Power BI Integration
* Integrated **Python Scripts** directly within the Power BI environment to leverage advanced statistical plotting that exceeds native BI visual capabilities.

---

## Key Insights (The "Story" of the Data)

1.  **The Victory of Science:** Measles incidence rates have plummeted by **96%** globally since the 1980s, showcasing the effectiveness of universal vaccination programs.
2.  **The Historical Shock:** Detected a massive **1993 reporting anomaly** in the US, highlighting how policy and definition changes can shift data trajectories.
3.  **Chronic Evolution:** AIDS has transitioned from an era of exponential growth to a stabilized plateau, indicating a shift from acute crisis management to long-term chronic care.
4.  **The Reporting Gap:** A strong correlation was found between infrastructure and data volume, identifying **Brazil and Mexico** as high-reporting hotspots.



---

## 💡 Strategic Recommendations
* **Shift Focus:** Transition healthcare funding from eradicated infectious diseases toward chronic condition management.
* **Infrastructure Investment:** Improve digital health reporting in under-represented regions to achieve a truly global epidemiological view.
* **Anomaly Detection:** Implement AI-driven early warning systems in dashboards to detect sudden surges in real-time.
